# Generic Crypto AML And AI Reporting

## Context

Use this story for AML, financial crime, AI reporting, risk analytics, compliance tooling, and interviews where the company needs product thinking around controls and decision support.

## Core facts

- Target narrative: AML and financial crime compliance product work in a regulated fintech or crypto environment.
- Source experience: AI/data product work plus compliance, risk, and data product patterns.
- Built or scoped AI/data prototypes for transaction analytics, anti-scam review, risk reporting, support operations, workflow automation, and trusted decision support.
- Designed LLM-powered reporting workflows across transaction, fund-flow, market-feed, user-behavior, and operational data.
- Sanitized impact framing: reduced manual reporting turnaround from hours to minutes.
- AML-oriented framing: suspicious fund-flow logic, alert rules, false-positive/false-negative tradeoffs, human review paths, auditability, and leadership-ready narratives.

## Interview value

This story helps explain how AI can support risk and compliance without replacing human judgment. It is useful when the interviewer cares about practical AI, operational review, AML monitoring, auditability, and adoption by non-technical teams.

## Reusable answer points

- Start with the control problem: manual risk reporting is slow, inconsistent, and hard to reuse for leadership or compliance decisions.
- Explain the product scope: define data inputs, alert logic, review checkpoints, explanation needs, quality criteria, and rollout risks.
- Show responsible AI judgment: keep human-in-the-loop review, log decisions, manage false positives, and make outputs explainable.
- Connect to payments: payments teams need fast, auditable visibility into suspicious activity, fund movement, failed payments, and operational exceptions.
