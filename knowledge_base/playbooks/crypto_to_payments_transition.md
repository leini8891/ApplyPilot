# Crypto To Payments Transition

## Context

Use this playbook when asked how crypto exchange, blockchain, or Web3 experience translates into mainstream payments and financial infrastructure.

## Core facts

- Relevant crypto/fintech work includes Bybit compliance dashboards, Binance product delivery, Lorenzo transaction-flow analytics, risk monitoring, fund-flow analysis, and AI reporting workflows.
- The bridge is not "crypto equals payments"; the bridge is regulated money movement, operational trust, user conversion, risk controls, data quality, and scalable platform workflows.
- The answer should be calm, practical, and free of hype.

## Interview value

This helps reposition crypto experience as disciplined fintech product experience. It prevents the conversation from getting stuck on whether the candidate has direct card acquiring ownership.

## Reusable answer points

- "I do not position crypto as the same as acquiring. I position it as adjacent financial infrastructure experience."
- "The transferable problems are transaction visibility, fund-flow risk, onboarding/KYC, suspicious activity monitoring, operational escalation, and customer trust."
- "Payments companies care about authorization, reliability, conversion, disputes, settlement, merchant operations, and risk controls. My prior work touches many of those patterns, even if the rails are different."
- "That gives me a strong base to learn the specific scheme, acquiring, and merchant details quickly."
