# Why This Role

## Context

Use this playbook for "Why this role?" in product manager interviews, especially when moving from crypto/data/enterprise backgrounds into payments.

## Core facts

- The role should be framed as a natural next step from payment-adjacent reliability, compliance operations, risk monitoring, and data product work.
- The candidate should acknowledge the domain transition directly and explain why the core product muscles transfer.
- Best supporting stories: consulting payment failure, generic crypto exchange KYC dashboard, DeFi analytics platform.

## Interview value

This answer reduces perceived career risk. It shows the move is intentional, not opportunistic, and gives the interviewer a clear reason to believe the transition can work.

## Reusable answer points

- "This role sits at the intersection I want: customer experience, technical platform workflows, risk controls, and measurable business outcomes."
- "My background is adjacent rather than identical, but I have repeatedly worked on the same product patterns: transaction reliability, exception handling, compliance visibility, data definitions, stakeholder workflows, and adoption."
- "I want a role where those patterns are not side projects, but the center of the product."
- "The learning curve is acquiring-specific details; the transferable strength is turning complex financial workflows into usable, reliable products."
